About Me

Who am I?

I'm Abdalla Ezzat. I have a huge passion for teaching and explaining concepts, as well as supporting students in understanding physics. From an early stage in my academic career, I have been dedicated to sharing my understanding of various concepts with others. I particularly enjoy the challenge of making abstract concepts, which often require a great deal of imagination, more accessible to those who struggle with them.

As a student researcher, I love exploring complex data and innovating solutions. With experience in several programming languages, including Python, C++, and FORTRAN, I've played a role in the ATLAS and FASER experiments at CERN's renowned Large Hadron Collider in Geneva, Switzerland.
